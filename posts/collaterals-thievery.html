the secret life of collaterals

april 5, 2023
·
collaterals
physiology
access thievery

Of all the pathophysiologic phenomena that I have come across in my medical training, the one that most readily continues to astound me is the human body's capacity for decompression.

The medical lexicon is saturated with terminology reflecting the abundance of pilfering in our biological workings. Aberrant blood cells are sequestered by the spleen; narrowed subclavian arteries steal flow from vertebrobasilar circulations; solid tumors co-opt and recruit

Perhaps, then, it is the unrelenting yet humble mission to bring this genre of thievery into view that draws me to radiology. The radiologist understands that what is visible is just that: an image that has been imagined and perceived.
